What is a Carbon Footprint?

A carbon footprint measures the total amount of greenhouse gases produced directly and indirectly by human activities, typically expressed in equivalent tons of carbon dioxide (CO2). It encompasses all sources of emissions, from transportation and energy use to food production and waste. Reducing your carbon footprint involves making conscious choices to minimize these emissions, such as using renewable energy, driving less, and recycling. Understanding and managing your carbon footprint is essential for combating climate change and protecting the environment.
